You are researching for the engineering team of Swaram, a voice-first PWA
that helps blind users in India scan and fill paper forms. The attached
document (SWARAM_SCAN_DOSSIER.txt) describes the entire current camera-scan
implementation: read it fully first — every recommendation must slot into
that architecture, respect its constraints (§11), preserve its verified
invariants (§5–§7), and attack its listed weaknesses (§10).

RESEARCH GOAL
Make Swaram's in-browser document detection, voice guidance, auto-capture,
and scan output quality world-class — on par with commercial scanner SDKs
(Scanbot SDK, Google ML Kit Document Scanner, Apple VisionKit/DataScanner,
Microsoft Lens, Genius Scan SDK, Dropbox's scanner) — WITHOUT native code or
per-scan licensing: everything must run in a browser PWA (Chrome Android
low-end + iOS Safari), preferably fully on-device. The unique twist: the
person aiming the camera is blind, so guidance and quality feedback are
delivered by voice.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER (in priority order)

1. HOW THE BEST SCANNER SDKS ACTUALLY WORK. Reverse-engineer from public
   sources (engineering blogs, patents, docs, conference talks, APK/model
   inspection write-ups) how Scanbot, ML Kit Document Scanner, VisionKit,
   Microsoft Lens, and Dropbox implement: document segmentation/corner
   detection (which model architectures — HED-style edge nets, U-Net/
   DeepLab segmentation, direct corner regression?), frame-to-frame
   tracking, auto-capture gating (their stability windows, angle/size/
   sharpness thresholds, hold timers), and capture-quality checks. What of
   this is replicable in a browser?

2. ON-DEVICE ML DOCUMENT DETECTION IN THE BROWSER. Survey open models and
   techniques that could replace or augment our four-path classical OpenCV
   detector (dossier §5) via onnxruntime-web (already a dependency):
   document-localization models (e.g. DocAligner, PaddleOCR's PP-Doc /
   UVDoc-adjacent models, LDC/HED edge detectors, MobileSAM-class
   segmentation, YOLO-style corner detectors trained on SmartDoc), their
   size, license, browser inference speed on low-end Android (WASM vs
   WebGL vs WebGPU backends), and conversion paths to ONNX. Recommend a
   specific model + backend + fallback plan, with expected FPS at ~480–640
   px input. If a well-trained classical pipeline can match ML on our
   scenes, say so with evidence.

3. CAMERA CAPTURE QUALITY IN THE BROWSER. Definitive current state (2025-26)
   of: ImageCapture.takePhoto() support and real resolution gains on
   Android Chrome; iOS Safari's actual ceiling (getUserMedia max
   resolution, ImageCapture/grabFrame support, known Safari bugs);
   MediaTrackConstraints for focusMode/focusDistance, exposureMode/
   exposureCompensation, pointsOfInterest, zoom, torch — which browsers
   honor them; requestVideoFrameCallback for the analysis loop; strategies
   SDK-quality web scanners use (burst capture + sharpest-frame selection,
   capture-time constraint renegotiation, OffscreenCanvas/worker
   pipelines). Give a concrete capture-path upgrade for our capture()
   (dossier §6, weakness W4/W5/W7).

4. REAL-TIME QUALITY GATES. Cheap-enough-for-500ms-ticks algorithms
   (classical or tiny-ML) for: glare/specular highlight detection, shadow
   detection, exposure problems, motion-blur prediction (frame differencing
   or DeviceMotion/gyro — including iOS permission flow), and adaptive
   sharpness thresholds that replace our fixed Laplacian-variance-40. How
   do commercial SDKs gate auto-capture on these? Which belong in the
   guidance ladder (dossier §4.6) and with what spoken feedback?

5. ACCESSIBLE (EYES-FREE) SCANNING UX. Research how blind users actually
   scan documents today: Microsoft Seeing AI's document channel, Apple
   VoiceOver + doc detection in Magnifier/Files, Envision AI, Be My Eyes.
   What guidance vocabularies, audio cues (earcons vs speech vs continuous
   sonification like pitch-for-distance), haptic patterns, and capture
   confirmations are proven to work? Evaluate our current guidance ladder
   and speech discipline (dossier §4.6–4.7) against this literature and
   propose concrete improvements (edge-specific cues, distance estimates,
   rotation guidance) with attention to speech latency.

6. OUTPUT ENHANCEMENT. Post-capture processing feasible on-device in the
   browser: curved-page dewarping (DewarpNet, DocTr, UVDoc, FDRNet — any
   browser-feasible distillations?), shadow removal, illumination
   normalization, the standard SDK enhancement profiles (original/
   grayscale/clean-BW/color-boost) and when to apply which for (a) OCR
   input and (b) the archived/printed PDF background; plus a cheap
   OCR-readability probe at confirm time so the app can SAY "the text
   looks sharp" (weakness W13) — e.g. a fast tesseract sample on a crop,
   or a text-region sharpness metric.

7. EVALUATION HARNESS. Extend our fake-camera approach (scripts/
   scan-sim.mjs, dossier §9): how to build a reproducible benchmark of
   hard scenes (glare, shadows, low contrast, occluding fingers, curved
   pages, busy Indian-household backgrounds) — synthetic generation plus
   public datasets (SmartDoc 2015/2017, DocUNet, WarpDoc, MIDV-500) —
   and which metrics to track per change: corner IoU / Jaccard, detection
   rate, time-to-auto-capture, false-capture rate, downstream OCR accuracy
   delta.

8. MULTI-PAGE & EDGE CASES (secondary). Patterns for multi-page capture
   sessions (SDK UX conventions), double-page spreads, ID-card-sized
   documents, and how they'd fit our voice-first flow.

DELIVERABLE FORMAT
- Executive summary: the 5 highest-leverage changes ranked by (capture
  quality gain x guidance/UX gain) / implementation effort, each mapped to
  the dossier weakness (W1–W15) it addresses.
- Per-question findings with citations to primary sources (SDK docs,
  papers, caniuse/web-platform status, model repos with licenses). Include
  concrete numbers: model sizes, FPS on representative hardware, browser
  support tables, benchmark scores.
- A proposed target architecture (text diagram) for the upgraded scan
  pipeline: analysis loop (worker/rVFC), detector (ML + classical
  fallback), tracker, quality gates, capture path, enhancement, and where
  each voice cue fires.
- A migration plan in 3 phases (quick wins <=1 week, medium <=1 month,
  ambitious), each phase shippable alone and testable with the fake-camera
  harness.
- Flag anything in the dossier's assumptions your research contradicts.

Be skeptical of vendor marketing; prefer measured/benchmarked claims; when
browser support claims matter (especially iOS Safari), cite current
platform-status sources rather than older blog posts, and say explicitly
when evidence is thin.
